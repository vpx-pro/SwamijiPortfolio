About the Maestro

A journey through classical Indian music

Musical Journey

Master Pandit Satyendra Deo Sharma began his musical training at the tender age of 8 under the guidance of the world-famous Maestro Baba Ustad Allaudin Khan. This early foundation in classical music shaped his destiny as one of India's most accomplished sitar performers.

Recognition & Achievements

His exceptional sitar performances earned him the prestigious title of "Little Ravishankar" from the Chief Minister, a testament to his mastery of the instrument and his ability to captivate audiences with his soulful renditions.

Spiritual Connection

Pandit Satyendra had the unique privilege of performing in the presence of the spiritual master OSHO for two years in Pune, India. This experience deepened his understanding of music as a spiritual practice and meditation.

The Art of Raga

Experience an energetically uplifting yet calming evening of classical sounds as Master Pandit Satyendra Deo Sharma gracefully plays the Sitar while singing in the ancient language of Sanskrit, based on the Raga tradition.

Divine Origins

Music created by deities Brahma and Saraswati, intended for a meditative, devotional, and entertaining experience for the human spirit.

Universal Appeal

Raga is pure music, requiring no religious affiliation, offering a peaceful and uplifting effect on the mind, body, and soul.

Spiritual Practice

Each performance is a journey of self-discovery and inner peace, connecting the performer and audience to the divine through sound.

Musical Legacy

In 1974, Master Pandit Satyendra Deo Sharma established a music school in Madhya Pradesh, India, dedicated to preserving and teaching the ancient art of classical Indian music. His commitment to education ensures that the rich traditions of Raga and sitar performance continue to inspire future generations.
